# YouTube Outlier Video Research Skill

**Triggers**: Human says "youtube outlier research", "find viral videos", "youtube video research", "analyze youtube formats", "hookpoint method", "youtube content strategy", "video format research".

**Purpose**: Systematically identify, analyze, and extract patterns from YouTube videos that have significantly outperformed expectations (high views relative to channel size) to inform content strategy and video creation.

---

## Overview

This skill teaches the complete **Hookpoint Method** for YouTube video research — a systematic process to find winning video formats, titles, thumbnails, and content structures that you can adapt for your own channel.

The core insight: **Outlier videos** (videos that get 10x-100x more views than the channel's average subscriber count) reveal what the algorithm and audience actually want, regardless of channel authority.

---

## The Hookpoint Research Process

### Phase 1: Define Your Research Parameters

Before searching, clarify:

1. **Your Niche/Topic**: What subject area are you researching?
   - Example: "AI automation for small business"
   
2. **Your ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**: Who are you trying to reach?
   - Example: "Non-technical business owners, 35-55, service-based businesses"

3. **Your Content Goals**: What do you want to achieve?
   - Brand awareness
   - Lead generation
   - Product education
   - Authority building

4. **Video Format Preferences**: What types of videos fit your brand?
   - Talking head
   - Screen share tutorials
   - Case studies
   - Listicles/roundups
   - Opinion/commentary

---

### Phase 2: Find Outlier Videos

#### Step 2.1: Search Strategy

**Method A: Topic-Based Search**
```
1. Go to YouTube
2. Search your core topic keywords
3. Filter by: Upload date (This Year or This Month)
4. Sort by: View count
5. Look for videos from small channels (<50k subs) with high views (100k+)
```

**Method B: Channel Deep-Dive**
```
1. Identify 10-20 competitor/adjacent channels in your niche
2. Go to their Videos tab
3. Sort by: Popular
4. Look for videos that outperform their subscriber count
5. Note: A 5k sub channel with a 500k view video = 100x outlier
```

**Method C: "Videos That Shouldn't Work"**
```
1. Search for your topic + "beginner", "how to", "tutorial"
2. Look for videos with:
   - Simple/low-production thumbnails
   - Basic titles (not clickbaity)
   - From unknown channels
   - But still have 100k+ views
3. These reveal pure topic demand, not production value
```

#### Step 2.2: Outlier Identification Criteria

A video qualifies as an **outlier** when:

| Metric | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| Views-to-Subscribers Ratio | >10x (ideally >50x) |
| Absolute Views | >100k (for research purposes) |
| Recency | Uploaded within last 12 months |
| Relevance | Directly serves your ICP |
| Format | Replicable for your channel |

**Red Flags (Skip These)**:
- Celebrity/influencer channels (built-in advantage)
- Heavily produced brand content (unreplicable budget)
- News/trending topics (time-sensitive, not evergreen)
- Controversy-driven views (not sustainable strategy)

### Phase 3: Analyze Outlier Patterns

For each outlier video, extract and document:

#### 3.1 Title Analysis

**Capture**:
- Exact title (copy/paste)
- Title length (character count)
- Title structure (question, how-to, list, statement, etc.)
- Keywords used
- Emotional triggers (curiosity, fear, desire, urgency)
- Numbers used (if any)

**Common Winning Title Patterns**:
```
- "How to [Achieve Result] Without [Common Obstacle]"
- "[Number] [Thing] That [Benefit]"
- "Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong"
- "The [Adjective] Way to [Desired Outcome]"
- "[Result] in [Timeframe] (Step by Step)"
- "I Tried [Thing] for [Timeframe] — Here's What Happened"
```

#### 3.2 Thumbnail Analysis

**Capture** (screenshot the thumbnail):
- Main visual element (person, object, text, graphic)
- Text on thumbnail (exact words, font style)
- Color scheme
- Facial expression (if person shown)
- Contrast level (high/low)
- Clarity at small size

**Common Winning Thumbnail Patterns**:
```
- High contrast colors (yellow/black, red/white)
- 3-5 words max on thumbnail
- Clear facial expression (surprise, excitement, concern)
- Before/after visual
- Arrow or circle highlighting key element
- Minimal text, maximum emotion
```

#### 3.3 Hook Analysis (First 30 Seconds)

**Watch and document**:
- Opening line (exact words)
- Hook type (question, statement, story, statistic, promise)
- Time to first value proposition
- Visual changes in first 30 seconds
- Music/audio style
- Pattern interrupts used

**Common Winning Hook Patterns**:
```
- "Most people think [X], but actually [Y]..."
- "I spent [time/money] testing [X] so you don't have to..."
- "Here's the thing nobody tells you about [topic]..."
- "If you're trying to [goal], stop doing [common mistake]..."
- "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to [result]..."
```

#### 3.4 Content Structure Analysis

**Map the video structure**:
```
0:00-0:30   Hook/Introduction
0:30-2:00   Problem agitations / Why this matters
2:00-5:00   Main teaching point #1
5:00-8:00   Main teaching point #2
8:00-10:00  Main teaching point #3
10:00-11:00 Summary / Key takeaways
11:00-12:00 Call-to-action
```

**Note**:
- Total video length
- Number of main points/sections
- Use of B-roll, screen shares, graphics
- Pacing (fast cuts vs. long takes)
- Story elements woven in

#### 3.5 Engagement Metrics

**Record**:
- View count
- Like count (calculate like-to-view ratio)
- Comment count (read top 10 comments)
- Comment sentiment (what are people saying?)
- Share indicators (if visible)

**Good Engagement Benchmarks**:
- Like-to-view ratio: >3% is strong
- Comment-to-view ratio: >0.5% is engaged
- Comments asking follow-up questions = high interest

### Phase 4: Pattern Synthesis

After analyzing 20-50 outlier videos, synthesize patterns:

#### 4.1 Create Pattern Categories

Group findings into:

**Title Patterns** (frequency count):
```
- How-to titles: 12 videos
- List titles: 8 videos
- Question titles: 6 videos
- Contrarian titles: 5 videos
```

**Thumbnail Patterns**:
```
- Face + text: 15 videos
- Before/after: 8 videos
- Screenshot/tutorial: 10 videos
- Graphic/illustration: 5 videos
```

**Hook Patterns**:
```
- Problem statement: 14 videos
- Story opening: 8 videos
- Statistic/shocking fact: 6 videos
- Direct promise: 10 videos
```

**Content Length**:
```
- 5-8 minutes: 8 videos
- 8-12 minutes: 18 videos
- 12-20 minutes: 10 videos
- 20+ minutes: 4 videos
```

#### 4.2 Identify "Golden Patterns"

Patterns that appear in >30% of outliers are **golden patterns** — these are your highest-probability formats to replicate.

Example synthesis:
```
GOLDEN PATTERNS IDENTIFIED:

1. Title: "How to [Result] Without [Obstacle]" — appeared in 40% of outliers
2. Thumbnail: Face with surprised expression + 3-word text overlay — 35% of outliers
3. Hook: Start with common misconception, then contradict — 45% of outliers
4. Length: 8-12 minutes — sweet spot for engagement
5. Structure: 3 main points with clear transitions — 60% of outliers
```

---

### Phase 5: Apply to Your Content

#### 5.1 Generate Video Ideas

Using the golden patterns, generate 10-20 video ideas:

**Template**:
```
TOPIC: [Your topic area]
GOLDEN PATTERN: [Pattern from research]
YOUR ADAPTATION: [Specific video idea]

Example:
TOPIC: AI automation for small business
GOLDEN PATTERN: "How to [Result] Without [Obstacle]"
YOUR ADAPTATION: "How to Add AI to Your Business Without Technical Skills"
```

#### 5.2 Score Each Idea

Rate each idea 1-10 on:

| Criteria | Weight | Score |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Matches golden title pattern | 20% | |
| Matches golden thumbnail pattern | 20% | |
| Matches golden hook pattern | 20% | |
| Serves your ICP directly | 25% | |
| Replicable with your resources | 15% | |

**Minimum score to proceed: 7/10**

#### 5.3 Create Content Brief

For each approved idea, create a brief:

```markdown
# Video Brief: [Title]

## Pattern Match
- Title pattern: [which golden pattern]
- Thumbnail pattern: [which golden pattern]
- Hook pattern: [which golden pattern]

## Target Metrics
- Expected views: [based on outlier benchmarks]
- Target length: [based on research]
- Target CTR: [based on thumbnail research]

## Content Outline
- Hook (0:00-0:30): [specific opening]
- Section 1 (0:30-X:XX): [main point]
- Section 2: [main point]
- Section 3: [main point]
- Summary + CTA: [closing]

## Production Notes
- Thumbnail concept: [description]
- B-roll needed: [list]
- Screen shares: [list]
- Graphics: [list]
```

## Weekly Research Routine

**Time commitment**: 2-3 hours per week

**Monday (60 min)**: Find 10-15 new outliers
- Search your core topics
- Check competitor channels
- Add to tracking sheet

**Wednesday (60 min)**: Analyze patterns
- Deep-dive on 5-7 outliers
- Extract title, thumbnail, hook patterns
- Update pattern synthesis

**Friday (30 min)**: Generate and score ideas
- Create 5-10 new video ideas from patterns
- Score each idea
- Add top 2-3 to content calendar

**Monthly Review (2 hours)**:
- Review which patterns are still working
- Identify emerging patterns
- Retire stale patterns
- Update golden patterns list

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Copying exact titles** — Adapt patterns, don't plagiarize
❌ **Ignoring your ICP** — Just because it worked for them doesn't mean it works for your audience
❌ **Analysis paralysis** — Don't research forever; ship videos
❌ **Only looking at big channels** — Focus on outliers from small channels
❌ **Ignoring recency** — YouTube changes fast; prioritize videos from last 12 months
❌ **One-and-done research** — Make this a weekly habit, not a one-time project

## Example: Complete Research Session

**Session Goal**: Find outlier videos about "AI automation for small business"

**Step 1**: Search YouTube
```
Search: "AI automation small business"
Filter: This Year
Sort: View count
```

**Step 2**: Identify outliers
```
Found: "How I Automated My Business with AI" 
- Channel: 8,400 subscribers
- Views: 847,000
- Ratio: 101x (OUTLIER ✓)
- Uploaded: 4 months ago
```

**Step 3**: Analyze
```
Title: "How I Automated My Business with AI (Save 20 Hours/Week)"
- Pattern: How-to + specific result + time benefit
- Length: 67 characters

Thumbnail: 
- Person with surprised expression
- Text: "20 HOURS SAVED"
- Red arrow pointing to calendar
- High contrast (yellow/black)

Hook (0:00-0:28):
"I used to work 60 hours a week running my business. 
Then I discovered these 3 AI tools. Now I work 30 hours 
and make more money. Let me show you exactly what I did."

Structure:
- 0:00-0:28: Hook with before/after
- 0:28-2:00: Problem (working too much)
- 2:00-5:00: Tool #1 (email automation)
- 5:00-8:00: Tool #2 (scheduling)
- 8:00-11:00: Tool #3 (customer service)
- 11:00-12:00: Summary + CTA

Engagement:
- 847k views, 34k likes (4.0% ratio ✓)
- 1,200 comments (0.14% ratio)
- Top comments: "Which tools?", "Trying this today"
```

**Step 4**: Add to tracking sheet
**Step 5**: Generate adapted idea
```
Your adaptation: "How to Add AI to Your Business This Weekend (No Coding Required)"
- Matches golden pattern: How-to + result + obstacle removal
- Serves ICP: Non-technical business owners
- Score: 8.5/10 ✓
```
