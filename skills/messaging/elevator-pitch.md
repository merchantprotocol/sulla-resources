# Elevator Pitch

This skill crafts an elevator pitch that makes people want to know more. The structure calls out the audience first (gets their attention), states the big desire (anchors their attention), mentions the big pain or problem they want to avoid, and finishes with the mechanism or product.

## Elevator Pitch Formula

```
I help [audience]
get [desire]
without [pain/wasted effort]
by [mechanism]
```

### Structure Breakdown

1. **"I help [audience]"** — Call out who you serve. This gets the right people's attention immediately.
2. **"get [desire]"** — State the ONE Big Desire from the empathy map. This is what anchors them.
3. **"without [pain]"** — Mention the big pain, problem, or prevailing knowledge they want to avoid. Sometimes calling out the big mistake works here.
4. **"by [mechanism]"** — Your USP, method, or product. This is what makes them ask "how?"

### Example

> I help people that owe the IRS reduce or settle their tax bill without going to jail by teaching them my method.

---

## Process

1. Pull the target audience, ONE Big Desire, ONE Big Problem/Pain, and USP from the empathy map.
2. Draft **3-5 variations** of the elevator pitch:
   - Vary which pain/problem you highlight (emotional pain vs. external problem vs. prevailing knowledge)
   - Vary the mechanism description (method vs. product vs. process)
   - Try both formal and conversational tones
3. Test each against: Would a stranger at a party lean in and ask "How do you do that?"
4. Recommend the strongest version.

## Rules

1. **The audience comes first.** If the right people don't hear themselves in the first few words, they tune out.
2. **Desire before pain.** Anchor them on what they want, then remind them what they're avoiding.
3. **The mechanism creates curiosity.** It should make them ask "how?" — not explain everything.
4. **Keep it under 30 seconds spoken.** If you can't say it in one breath, it's too long.
