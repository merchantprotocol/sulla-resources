# Complete Brand Script

This skill crafts the complete brand script — the whole story of the company. This is the full buyer's journey narrative. Use it on about pages, directory listings, long-form descriptions, and anywhere customers stop and read.

The message can be mixed and matched to focus on different parts of the journey.

## Brand Script Formula

```
At (company), we know you want (desire). In order to do that, you need (solution).

The problem is (external problem). This makes you feel (emotional pain). Nobody wants (fear).

We believe (empathy). That's why we (authority).

Here's our process:
1. (Start with the Bribe)
2. (Our main product)
3. (Big Result)

Get started (Bribe), and (First call to paid action). To stop (the failure) and instead (have the desire).
```

### Section Breakdown

1. **Opening — Desire + Solution:** Immediately acknowledge what the customer wants and what they need to get it. This tells them you understand.

2. **Problem — External + Emotional + Fear:** Agitate. State the real problem, how it makes them feel, and what they're afraid of. This is where the empathy map does its work.

3. **Empathy + Authority:** Show you understand (empathy statement) and prove you can deliver (authority/credentials). This builds trust.

4. **Process — 3 Steps:** Make it simple. Three steps from where they are to where they want to be. Step 1 is the low-risk entry point (bribe/lead magnet). Step 2 is the core product/service. Step 3 is the transformation/result.

5. **Call to Action:** Close with the bribe (free entry point), the paid action, and restate what they're avoiding vs. what they're getting.

---

## Process

1. Pull every element from the empathy map: desire, solution, external problem, emotional pain, fear, empathy statement, authority, USP.
2. Draft the brand script following the formula above.
3. Write **2-3 variations**:
   - **Full version** — for about pages, long-form content
   - **Short version** — condensed to 2-3 paragraphs for listings/directories
   - **Modular pieces** — each section as a standalone block that can be mixed/matched
4. Identify what the "bribe" (free entry point) should be — this will feed the lead magnet skill later.

## Rules

1. **Every element comes from the empathy map.** Don't invent claims.
2. **The formula works because of the order.** Desire → Problem → Trust → Process → CTA. Don't rearrange.
3. **The bribe is critical.** If you can't identify a clear free entry point, flag it — the lead magnet skill will need to solve this.
4. **Write in the brand's voice.** Use language and tone consistent with how the owner talks about their business.
5. **The modular pieces are as important as the full script.** Most marketing uses fragments, not the whole thing.
