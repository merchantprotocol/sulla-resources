# Mission Statement

This skill crafts a mission statement. It can be the mission of the company, the mission of the employees (culture), or the mission of the customers. Any angle works — pick the one that resonates most with the brand's voice and values.

---

## Input

Read:
- `~/sulla/identity/business/business-owner-perspective-empathy-map.md`
- `~/sulla/identity/business/onboarding.md`
- `~/sulla/identity/business/brand-script.md` (if available)

---

## Mission Statement Formulas

### Formula 1: Problem Elimination
```
A world without (problem)
```

### Formula 2: Audience Achievement
```
One day (audience) will get (desire)
```

### Formula 3: Empathy + Desire
```
(empathy) so they can achieve (desire)
```

---

## Process

1. Pull the problem, audience, desire, and empathy statement from the empathy map.
2. Generate **one version per formula** (3 total).
3. Generate **2-3 additional variations** that blend formulas or take a unique angle based on the owner's voice and story.
4. Determine which angle fits best:
   - **Company mission** — what the company exists to do
   - **Employee/culture mission** — what the team rallies around
   - **Customer mission** — what customers are trying to achieve (and you're enabling)
5. Recommend the strongest version and explain why.

## Rules

1. **Mission statements are short.** One sentence. Maybe two. Never a paragraph.
2. **Use the owner's conviction.** The empathy map raw quotes often contain the most authentic version of the mission.
3. **It has to be believable.** Don't write something grandiose that doesn't match the business's actual scope.
4. **All three angles are valid.** Don't force one — pick what fits the brand.
